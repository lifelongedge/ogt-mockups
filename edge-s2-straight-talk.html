No gloss. The real version.

Edge S2 is a complete trading operation you change with AI instead of waiting on a developer.

This page lays out what Edge S2 actually is, what it asks of you, what's solid today, and where it's still maturing, so you can decide with your eyes open.

If you've been burned by a developer you couldn't fire, or won over by an AI you can't fully trust, this page is written for you. Read it before you read the price.

3FRAME
Read this first

Three plain statements.

Everything else on this page hangs off these. If any one of them is a dealbreaker, we'd rather you know now.

What it is

A technology platform that lets you use AI to assist your trading: backtesting concepts, ideas, and real systems, evaluating your current system, running daily signals across multiple systems, and telling you what is working right now — EOD, and real-time for intraday. It gives you the mechanism to hook up your data and to use charts, indicators, and alerts as they are, or radically change them with AI.

What it isn't

It works inside firm limits, and those limits are the point.

  • It never places a live order — the trigger is always yours.
  • The AI proposes; you review and approve.
  • You bring your own market-data and AI keys.
What it costs you

A real monthly fee, a few hours to learn the workbench, and the discipline to review what the AI builds. The more you want to own and customize, the more technical comfort it asks for.

5THE AI QUESTION
The thing that burned you

"I like the AI. I don't trust it. Why is this different?"

A general chatbot hallucinates because it guesses from the open internet with nothing to check itself against. Edge S2 runs the AI inside a system designed to keep it honest, and this section is candid about where it still falls short.

Why it hallucinates less here

  • Grounded in your own data. It answers from your actual quotes, fills, and the platform's documented contracts.
  • Facts only. It's instructed to state numbers it actually computed and looked at, and to label anything speculative as speculation.
  • It shows the math. Core calculations are pinned to blessed ground-truth values, so you can see how an indicator is computed.
  • Propose, then approve. The AI drafts a change, and nothing touches your platform until you review and accept it.
  • Tests catch the breakage. A wrong change trips a ground-truth alarm or a contract check before it can ship. The guardrails are the safety net.

Where it still falls short

  • It still makes mistakes. The value is that its claims are checkable and its changes are gated before they ship.
  • It can misread your intent. A vague request gets a literal answer. Clear questions get better results, and that's a skill you build over time.
  • You stay accountable. It's an assistant. Your trades and your decisions are always yours.
  • Big changes need real review. The deeper the customization, the more your judgment (or our support) matters. Expect to supply the judgment on anything substantial.

7NO MORE HOSTAGE
The scar this is built for

You were held hostage by a developer once. Here's how this is structured so it doesn't happen again.

What changes

  • You stop hiring the bottleneck. A new scan or indicator is a plain-English request you review the same day you ask, with no ticket queue.
  • The math is visible and pinned. Every component documents its own interface, so a change tells you exactly what it touches.
  • Self-hosted means you hold the source. No one can switch it off, lock you out, or hold your studies for ransom.
  • Your work outlives our updates. What you build lives in your own space and survives upgrades to the core.

What stays

  • You may still want help, and that's allowed. Persona 1 should lean on the managed tier and our support rather than become a reluctant engineer.
  • The AI is real leverage, and it still needs your sign-off. It does the developer's typing against guardrails while your judgment approves the result.
  • We earn the renewal every month. Because you hold the source, we keep you by shipping updates, support, and data.
  • We tell you when something isn't ready. The maturity table above is part of that. The candor is the relationship.
8WHAT YOU BRING
No hidden dependencies

What you supply, in plain terms.

Market data

You bring your own feed, and it stays yours. End-of-day history for liquid US stocks and ETFs is inexpensive and simple — that's all Persona 1 needs. Intraday needs a real-time feed (Persona 2 and 3).

The institutional sector/breadth modules need a specific vendor feed; if you don't have it, you run the large core that doesn't, and those modules stay off until you add it.

AI key & your machine

You bring your own AI key, so your spend is yours and your conversations aren't pooled with anyone else's. Managed (Cloud) runs on our hardware as a single isolated instance; self-hosted runs entirely on yours, where your data never leaves.

No part of this quietly phones home with your fills, your watchlists, or your journal.

11NOT FOR YOU IF

12HARD QUESTIONS

Straight answers for the interested but skeptical

What happens to me if you disappear?

On self-hosted you hold the full source and the platform runs on your machine, so a vendor outage or shutdown doesn't switch you off. On managed, your data and studies are yours and exportable. The honest risk in any small-vendor relationship is pace of updates and support — which is exactly why self-hosted exists for the people who can't tolerate that risk.

I'm not technical. Will I actually be able to use this?

Yes, on the managed tier. The workbench, charts, scans, and the chat assistant are point-and-click and plain English. You won't open a code editor. What you gain is the ability to vet an idea yourself instead of trusting a proposal or a confident-but-wrong chatbot. What you won't gain is the ability to rewrite the platform's internals — and you won't need to.

I'm fairly technical. Will it work for me?

This is where the platform shows its depth. Self-hosted ships completely open: full source, a contract-first codebase where every component declares its interface, and extension points that survive upgrades so your work lives in your own space. It runs like hardened infrastructure underneath — validated daily data, no-look-ahead by construction, ground-truth pinned to blessed values, monitoring, backups, and regression tests. The AI then works as a force-multiplier over a documented codebase, so you extend a running operation instead of wiring one up. One honest caveat: if you'd rather spend that time building the whole stack yourself from scratch, this is not for you. The value here is the hardened, documented platform that already exists.

Can I really trust the AI with my trading decisions?

Trust it to research, frame, and check your work. The decision stays yours. It's grounded in your data and the platform's pinned ground-truth, instructed to state only what it computed, and every change it proposes is reviewed before it lands. It still makes mistakes, which is why the guardrails and your sign-off exist. Treat it as a sharper second opinion.

Why does it cost what it costs when AI is getting cheap?

You're paying for the hardened operation around the AI — the validated daily data, the no-look-ahead backtesting, the pinned ground-truth, the monitoring and backups, and the support — plus ongoing updates. The AI key spend is yours and separate. The price is for the system that keeps the AI honest and the data correct, which is the part that's hard to build.

What actually breaks, and how often?

The bedrock — EOD data, charts, backtests, scans on liquid names — is the most battle-tested part and rarely surprises. The newer surfaces (broad self-host packaging, the institutional-signal modules, multi-tenant cloud) are where rough edges live, which is why the maturity table grades them honestly. Early self-host customers get hands-on onboarding for precisely this reason.

How is this different from just using ChatGPT plus TradingView?

Edge S2 gives you two things together that a chatbot and a charting site can't: an AI grounded in your own data and the platform's documented rules, and backtesting that can't look ahead by construction. A general chatbot guesses and can't check itself. A charting site shows you a price and leaves the question of whether your system is reproducibly profitable unanswered. Edge S2 closes both gaps in one stack.

Does this replace my current platform?

Probably not in the short term, and you'll have to decide for yourself. We've found that our own reliance on supplementary tools like AmiBroker, TradeStation, and TradingView has declined, and we still keep those tools around. They have a place if you've worked with them for a long time. Our hope is that Edge S2 becomes an indispensable tool for you.
