The 100 Tips — Getting Started at a glance

7 categories × 5 stages (Novice → Master). Categories 5–7 are universal disciplines — they apply at every stage, so they span the full row.

Category Novice Apprentice Journeyman Expert Master
1
PLANNING
Building the Map Before the Journey
1
Define your why.
Write your reason for trading on paper before risking a single dollar. Without purpose, pressure breaks you.
2
Choose one market.
Stop scanning everything. Pick one instrument and learn it deeply. Mastery begins with focus.
3
Write the plan.
If it is not written, it does not exist. Open a document today and start your first trading plan.
4
Study before screens.
Read one foundational book before placing trades. Knowledge compounds faster than losses.
5
Accept slow growth.
You are not behind. You are early. Begin where you are without apology.
6
Define your edge.
Name the specific condition where you have statistical advantage. If you cannot describe it, you do not have one yet.
7
Map your timeframe.
Decide whether you are a scalper, day trader, swing trader, or position trader. Stop trading across all of them.
8
Set capital limits.
Decide today the maximum capital you are willing to lose entirely. Anything beyond that is not risk capital.
9
Plan your week.
Schedule study, review, and trading sessions in advance. Improvisation guarantees inconsistency.
10
Define success metrics.
Track process metrics, not just profit. Measure what you can control before measuring what you cannot.
11
Build playbook depth.
Document every setup you trade with entry, stop, target, and invalidation. A trader without a playbook is a gambler with vocabulary.
12
Identify regime shifts.
Markets behave differently in trends, ranges, and volatility expansions. Plan strategies for each.
13
Stress test plans.
Imagine your worst week. Plan how you will respond before it arrives.
14
Schedule strategic reviews.
Block calendar time monthly for full system review. What is not scheduled does not get improved.
15
Plan exit conditions.
Decide in advance what would cause you to stop trading a strategy. Without exit criteria, you will ride dead methods into the ground.
16
Design portfolio architecture.
Allocate capital across uncorrelated strategies. Concentration is a tax on maturity.
17
Build redundancy layers.
Backup systems, backup brokers, backup internet. Failure visits prepared traders less violently.
18
Plan tax efficiency.
Trading without tax planning is bleeding profit silently. Engage professionals annually.
19
Model worst case.
Run scenarios assuming 30% drawdowns. If you cannot survive them mentally and financially, restructure now.
20
Plan capacity limits.
Strategy returns degrade with size. Know your ceiling before reaching it.
21
Design legacy systems.
Build structures that outlast your active career. Mastery is measured in what survives you.
22
Plan generational wealth.
Trade not just for income but for inheritance. The long journey serves more than yourself.
2
PREPARATION
Strengthening the Body, Mind, and Systems
23
Sleep is alpha.
Seven hours minimum. Tired traders make terrible decisions. Protect sleep aggressively.
24
Hydrate before trading.
Dehydration reduces cognitive performance measurably. Drink water before opening the chart.
25
Eat for focus.
Heavy meals dull judgment. Eat clean before market hours.
26
Clear the environment.
Remove distractions from your workspace. Visual noise becomes cognitive noise.
27
Practice patience daily.
Sit quietly five minutes before the open. Stillness precedes execution.
28
Journal before sessions.
Write your mental state, biases, and intentions before trading. Awareness precedes discipline.
29
Review yesterday first.
Never start a session without reviewing the prior day. Memory without review becomes story.
30
Pre-mark levels.
Identify key support, resistance, and reaction zones before the open. Decisions in real time should already be partially made.
31
Practice physical readiness.
Stretch, walk, breathe. The body is the platform of the mind.
32
Limit caffeine spikes.
Caffeine masks fatigue but does not replace rest. Use sparingly.
33
Rehearse mentally.
Visualize executing setups before market open. Mental rehearsal builds neural pathways.
34
Prepare contingency responses.
Decide in advance how you will respond to gaps, news shocks, and unexpected volatility.
35
Calibrate position sizing.
Adjust size based on volatility, not emotion. Volatility is the speed of risk.
36
Maintain technical hygiene.
Update software, check feeds, test alerts daily. Equipment failure is preventable failure.
37
Protect deep work.
Block social media during analysis sessions. Attention is the rarest professional asset.
38
Train physical resilience.
Trading is endurance. Exercise four times weekly minimum. Mental stamina sits on physical foundation.
39
Manage cognitive load.
Reduce non-trading decisions during market hours. Decision fatigue is real and expensive.
40
Build recovery rituals.
Schedule decompression after sessions. Unprocessed stress accumulates as bias.
41
Cultivate boredom tolerance.
Most preparation is repetitive. Mastery requires loving the unglamorous middle.
42
Audit information diet.
Eliminate noisy sources. Signal quality determines decision quality.
43
Preserve nervous system.
Years of trading tax the body. Prioritize long-term physiological maintenance.
44
Steward mental clarity.
Protect your mind as you would protect capital. Both compound when guarded.
3
EXECUTION
Acting Under Pressure
45
Set the stop.
Never enter without a predefined stop loss. Trading without stops is donating capital.
46
Honor the rails.
Once rules are set, follow them. Negotiating with yourself mid-trade is the beginning of ruin.
47
Trade small first.
Size up only after consistency. Confidence is earned, not assumed.
48
Accept being wrong.
Losses are tuition. Pay them quickly and stay enrolled.
49
Press the button.
Hesitation kills setups. When the plan triggers, execute.
50
Risk one percent.
Never risk more than 1% of account on a single trade. Survival precedes performance.
51
Trade your plan.
Not the news. Not the chat room. Not the feeling. Only the plan.
52
Walk away winning.
After a strong session, stop. Greed gives back gifts the market rarely offers twice.
53
Cut losses quickly.
Small losses keep the door open. Large losses close it.
54
Avoid revenge trades.
A loss is information, not an insult. Do not chase the market for emotional restitution.
55
Scale into conviction.
Add to winners, not losers. Average up, not down.
56
Trust the setup.
If conditions match the playbook, execute without second-guessing. Doubt belongs in review, not execution.
57
Manage the middle.
Most trades fail in the middle, not at entry or exit. Predefine management rules.
58
Respect the regime.
Trade trend strategies in trends, mean reversion in ranges. Forcing methods on wrong regimes destroys edges.
59
Embrace incomplete information.
You will never have full clarity. Act on probability, not certainty.
60
Execute mechanically.
Discretion within structure, never structure within discretion.
61
Honor stop hierarchy.
Time stops, technical stops, volatility stops. Each protects against a different failure mode.
62
Detach from outcome.
Judge execution by process adherence. Outcome is a lagging indicator.
63
Reduce after drawdown.
Cut size during cold streaks. Survival mode preserves opportunity for the warm streak ahead.
64
Increase only after consistency.
Scale up only after sustained profitable consistency. Premature scaling magnifies weakness.
65
Operate from stillness.
The deepest execution comes from quiet presence, not aggressive force.
66
Move with the market.
Do not impose. Respond. The market leads. The master follows precisely.
4
ASSESSMENT
Learning from Every Step
67
Review every trade.
Win or lose, study it. Each chart is a teacher if you bother to listen.
68
Screenshot every setup.
Build a visual library of your own decisions. Pattern recognition develops through repetition.
69
Name the mistake.
If you broke a rule, write which rule and why. Vague reflection produces vague improvement.
70
Track emotional state.
Note your feelings before, during, and after each trade. Emotion is data, not weakness.
71
Celebrate process wins.
Did you follow the plan? That is the real victory. Outcome is secondary to discipline.
72
Score the session.
Rate execution quality from one to ten daily. Numbers make patterns visible.
73
Separate luck from skill.
A winning trade made on broken rules is a dangerous teacher. Punish bad process even when it pays.
74
Find repeating errors.
Most traders make the same five mistakes endlessly. Identify yours and attack them directly.
75
Compare planned to actual.
Where did execution diverge from intention? That gap is where growth lives.
76
Weekly written review.
End every week with a written summary. Reflection without writing fades within hours.
77
Quantify your edge.
Calculate win rate, average win, average loss, expectancy. Feelings lie. Numbers do not.
78
Audit by setup type.
Some setups carry your edge. Some bleed quietly. Cut what does not earn its place.
79
Review drawdown behavior.
Study how you behave during losing streaks. That is your true trading character.
80
Conduct quarterly reviews.
Zoom out. Patterns invisible weekly become obvious quarterly.
81
Track behavioral drift.
Are you sizing up emotionally? Holding too long? Skipping stops? Drift kills accounts slowly.
82
Benchmark against self.
Compare yourself to your own twelve-month trailing performance, not to others.
83
Stress test results.
Strip out your best month. Strip out your luckiest trade. What remains is your real edge.
84
Audit risk exposure.
Measure correlation between positions. Hidden concentration is hidden danger.
85
Examine identity drift.
Are you still trading the strategy you claim to trade? Identity erodes quietly under pressure.
86
Conduct annual postmortems.
A full year reviewed honestly reveals truths no week can show.
87
Teach to deepen mastery.
Explaining your craft to others exposes weak reasoning. Teaching is advanced learning.
88
Refine the system continuously.
No edge lasts forever. Markets adapt. Masters adapt faster.
5
EMOTIONAL REGULATION
The Inner Game
89
Breathe before reacting.
Four seconds in, four seconds out. The pause between stimulus and response is where mastery lives.
90
Name the emotion.
Fear, greed, frustration, euphoria. Labeling reduces emotional grip immediately.
91
Step away first.
When triggered, leave the screen. No good decision is made in emotional flood.
92
Sleep on big choices.
Major strategic decisions deserve overnight processing. Urgency is usually illusion.
93
Forgive yourself quickly.
Mistakes are tuition. Self-punishment is interest you do not owe.
94
Detach identity from trades.
You are not your P&L. You are the trader executing the process behind it.
6
RISK MANAGEMENT
The Sacred Discipline
95
Survival is strategy.
Stay in the game long enough and edges compound. Blow up once and the journey ends.
96
Risk fixed amounts.
Predefined, consistent risk per trade. Variable risk produces variable mindset.
97
Hard stops always.
Mental stops are negotiable stops. Negotiable stops become catastrophic stops.
98
Daily loss limits.
When the limit hits, close the platform. The market will be there tomorrow. Make sure you are too.
7
CONTINUOUS IMPROVEMENT
The Long Journey
99
Improve one percent.
Small daily improvements compound into mastery. The mountain is climbed in inches.
100
Stay the course.
You will fail. You will doubt. You will want to quit. Do not. The traders who arrive are simply the ones who did not stop walking.
