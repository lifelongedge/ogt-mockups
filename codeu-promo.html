Anatomy of a lab

Each lab is a loop. Not a video.

Most courses show you correct code and hope it lands. codeu hands you the running system and dares you to break it. You feel the failure, then you fix it. That loop is the whole product.

03

04

Fix

5THE CLIMB
The value ladder

Mastery isn't a course. It's a climb.

The labs are tagged by level, but the levels don't gate you — they tell you where you are. Here's what each rung means you can actually do.

NEWCOMERday one

You can read the codebase, ship a safe change, and explain why your edit didn't show up. The cache-bust ritual, the field-name traps, the time gotchas — internalized before they cost you a day.

BUILDERshipping

You add an indicator that flows into every consumer, register a new view, and drive a job through its lifecycle. You know what breaks and you break it on purpose first.

MAINTAINERowning

You walk a cross-repo blast radius before you rename anything, handle nullable fields defensively, and respect the golden-file gate. You keep the platform green.

ARCHITECTdesigning

You reason about storage-vs-API shape, working-set tiers, no-look-ahead windows, and multi-timeframe broadcast. You make the decisions the earlier rungs depend on.

PRINCIPALmastering

You direct AI against a contract-bound codebase and catch what it missed, design daemons that fail loud and recover, and set the conventions everyone else follows.

Every lab is a real decision from a platform that supports real traders in live market conditions — the quote store, the contract discipline, the daily cycle that can't miss. Genericized so the idea travels to whatever you build, but never invented for a classroom.

Built from scratch with AI — by necessity

This platform was built solo, from zero, with AI doing most of the typing. We couldn't have built it otherwise. The course shows what that actually took: the contracts, guardrails, and review discipline that make AI produce software that holds up in production instead of falling apart in week two.

The decisions, not just the syntax

Aspiring system developers can already write a function. What they're missing is the judgment — store-vs-reconstruct, contract-first, no-look-ahead, fail-loud. These are the calls that separate code that runs from systems that survive, and they're what every lab is about.

You don't watch the bug, you cause it, then fix it — in a live editor, the bigger half of every screen, no install or account. The lesson is muscle memory by the time you close the tab.

9FAQ
FAQ

Questions.

Do I need access to the platform it's based on?

No. Every lab runs entirely in your browser on synthetic, curated data. You never touch the real system, and you need no credentials, server, or repo clone.

Is this for beginners or experienced engineers?

Both. The labs are tagged Newcomer → Principal. A first-week developer starts at the bottom of a track; a senior engineer skips to the Architect and Principal labs. Nothing is gated — the levels just tell you where you are.

Do I need to know trading?

No. The platform happens to be a trading system, but the labs teach engineering — storage design, contracts, time handling, scheduling, AI review. Any domain knowledge you pick up is a bonus.

Can I use AI to get through the labs?

Please do. codeu is built for how developers actually learn now — there's a grounded AI seat in every lab, and an entire track on directing AI well. Using AI is the skill, not the cheat.

What do I get for the price?

Lifetime access to the labs in your tier, every future lab added to those tracks for free, challenge gates, progress tracking, and a completion certificate on the Full Course. The Full Course is a one-time payment.

Refunds?

30 days, no questions asked. If the first few labs don't change how you think about building software, email us and we refund it.
