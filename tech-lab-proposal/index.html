Tech Lab — How we make it stick, wow, and get shared

Eight ideas to take the Tech Lab from a clean course to one traders compete to finish and post about. Each idea is illustrated below. Idea 5 is interactive — your vote actually counts toward the prioritization.

1. Personal symbol 2. Owl-says vs You-say 3. Receipt cards 4. Find it in your platform 5. Cohort vote · live 6. Diamond Mind streak 7. Claude hooks 8. Referral URLs Ship 3 first
1Personal-symbol injection
Stickiness · in-lab

The Fractal Stack symbol picker today is a hardcoded list. Let the trader paste their own ticker. The aha moment is not the math — it is seeing AAPL, or the dog they actually own, through the framework for the first time.

RL10 vs RL270 excursion +1.7σ stretched · floodplain

Why this matters

Every learner has 5–10 tickers they keep coming back to. Demo symbols are forgettable. Their own portfolio is not.

The data-source path already exists: fetchLive in lab-engine.js + the EOD WarmDB endpoint. Wire it through Panel 1 and Panel 3 first.

Effort: ~1 day · Reach: every learner · Risk: low

2Owl-says vs You-say · gut-vs-framework gap
Wow moment · self-test

Before each panel reveals its number, the trader guesses. One click. Then the framework reveals. By the third lesson the trader has a personal calibration score — that is the screenshot they post.

Right now, where is SPY sitting?
Calibration so far · 0 / 0 correct

Why this matters

Passive watching does not stick. Forced commitment before reveal does. This is the cheapest learning-science move available.

Cumulative score "you over-call Max Pain 60% of the time" is a personal stat — and a tweet.

Effort: ~2 days for 9 panels · Reach: every learner · Risk: low

7Claude hooks · five concrete moves
AI integration · already wired

The Tech Lab already has an "Ask AI" button (TechuChat). These five behaviours weaponize it.

Explain like I am new

One toggle on every dense aside. Claude rewrites the paragraph at the trader's stated experience level. Cached per (panel, level).

"The Dragon is a smoothed regression line wrapped in a half-σ envelope" → "Think of it as a tight rubber band that hugs the short-term trend."

Why did this fail?

Trader pastes a losing trade — entry, stop, exit, symbol. Claude walks through which RLFF frame would have flagged it. The killer feature.

"You entered TSLA at +2.4σ. Panel 3 says the prior 47 occurrences reverted to SMA30 within 5 days 78% of the time. Your entry was on the wrong side of the river."

Voice-mode Ask AI

Voice infra already in place (/home/egyre/edge-transcriptions/). Trader scrubs the chart, talks through what they see, Claude responds in voice. Feels like Ken on the phone.

End-of-lesson receipt

Three personalized takeaways based on which panels they actually moved sliders on. Becomes the Receipt Card body (idea 3) — lesson-scope rather than panel-scope.

Claude-graded homework

Trader submits a TC2000 screenshot of a Morning Hook. Claude reads it (vision), checks geometry against the rules, gives one-line feedback + Owl/Frog/Tortoise stamp. Closes the loop with idea 4.

8Referral URLs baked into every share
Conversion · 5–10× generic links

Each shareable card carries a unique link s2.lifelongedge.com/r/<lesson>/<handle>. The new visitor lands on a clone of the same panel, pre-loaded with the same symbol and parameter values. They get to play with the exact moment that made their friend share.

https://s2.lifelongedge.com/r/river-dragon/sundeep?sym=AAPL&z=2.3
→ Loads Panel 3 with AAPL, scrubbed to the bar at Z = +2.3σ, Owl gauge already pinned. Visitor can drag, change symbols, or sign up to save their session.

Almost free to build because the panel state already lives in the URL hash. Add tracking + redirect rule. Done.
