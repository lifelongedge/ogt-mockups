Sniper

Sniper is Ken Long’s name for short-timeframe, ruthlessly-managed intraday trades taken on a single high-volatility target using a small, well-defined setup (often an opening-range break) with a tight risk box.

Ken’s framing

Ken uses “Sniper Trade of the Day” (STOD) for the single best intraday trade he walks through each evening on the podcast — typically a 1-minute or 3-minute chart trade on one volatile target, entered on an opening-range break (OR3) or simple sniper setup, managed with a minimum-manageable risk box, and stop-and-reversed without ego when the trade fails. In the live-trading-stats review (yt 7NOBcX9qoPE) Ken frames the entire style: “100 trades per week … 1,467 trades, 60% win rate, average win 1R / average loss 0.4R, expectancy 0.48R with SD 0.95 — that’s how we manufacture our edge.” In the AAPL turbo lesson (yt 3WUJTysI1LQ) he distinguishes Sniper trading as the “turbo” engine that funds the “core” swing position — lower-risk because there’s no overnight gap to contend with.

Why it matters

Sniper is the unit of deliberate practice that turns a swing-trader into a complete trader: every Sniper trade is a full rep of pattern recognition, risk-box construction, entry timing, scoop management, stop-and-reverse discipline, and exit decision compressed into minutes instead of days. The 60% win-rate / 2.5-to-1 payoff distribution Ken documents only emerges when the same handful of setups (OR3 breakout, Kata-2 entry, PSAR flip, simple sniper setup) are executed ruthlessly across hundreds of attempts. Without a Sniper practice, the swing-trader has no fast feedback loop and no way to manufacture an edge from intraday volatility.

Try it yourself

Pick one liquid, volatile name from tomorrow’s RLCO/tactical target list (e.g., TSLA, NVDA, or SPY itself) and commit to running one Sniper Trade of the Day on it. Before the open, write down (1) the timeframe you’ll use (1-min or 3-min), (2) the setup you’ll wait for (OR3 break, Kata-2 entry, or simple sniper setup off PSAR), and (3) the exact risk-box placement that defines your -1R. Take the trade live or in simulation, and after the close write a 5-line podcast-style recap in Ken’s voice: setup → entry → management → exit → R-multiple. Do this for 20 sessions and tabulate your win rate, average win, average loss, and expectancy — then compare against Ken’s documented 60% / 1R / -0.4R / 0.48 expectancy benchmark.
