Frog

An intraday trading system that uses descriptive statistics — specifically a volatility “Frog box” (30-day range stddev) — to separate signal from noise and find symbols poised to make larger-than-normal moves.

Ken’s framing

Per the SGC rlco_notes: “The Frog system is our design effort to reduce intraday trading to a simple system that requires the minimum amount of information to take effective action — a low risk idea with positive expectancy that can be realistically traded across multiple market types. The frog relies on descriptive statistics to help us define the difference between signal and noise.” In Ken’s video framing, the Frog box is “the standard deviation of the 30-day range… a measure of volatility” used as the non-noise signal threshold. Frog 1.0 risked one full Frog box; Frog 2.0 / hybrid Frog compresses that to roughly one-third (the MMRB / R10), entering after only 0.3–0.4 of a Frog box of confirmation so risk can be moved to “no-lose-plus-dinner-for-two” before a Frog 1.0 trader would have even entered.

Why it matters

Frog is the conceptual ancestor of nearly everything else in Ken’s day-trading toolkit — MMRB, R10, the Sniper, and the Compound Critical States lens. Earning its own page teaches the student to think in volatility-normalized units (boxes) rather than dollars or percentages, which is what makes positive-expectancy intraday trading possible across symbols and market regimes. Understanding “what makes a good Frog candidate” is shorthand for understanding what makes any symbol poised for a larger-than-normal move tomorrow.

Try it yourself

Pick five symbols you traded or watched yesterday. For each, compute (or look up) the 30-day range standard deviation — that’s its Frog box. Then for today’s session, write down: (a) the Frog quality number (avg-range divided by Frog box — is it greater than 3?), (b) the signal-to-noise ratio of yesterday’s candle (|close-open| / (high-low)), and (c) any Sniper setups in play. Now rank the five by how strongly each is “poised to make a larger-than-normal move.” Trade-paper one of them tomorrow using an MMRB-sized stop (≈ Frog box / 3) and journal whether the candidate’s behavior matched your ranking. The goal is to feel the difference between a high-Frog-quality compressed symbol and a noisy one — in your gut, not just on paper.
