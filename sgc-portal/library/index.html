SGC Concept Library

These five concepts form the core of Ken Long’s intraday trading framework — read them in order. Start with PPEA, the four-phase process (Plan, Prepare, Execute, Assess) that governs every trading decision. Then learn the Frog system, which uses descriptive statistics on the 30-day range to find symbols poised to move, and the MMRB, the noise-aware stop sizing derived from that same statistic. Finish with RLCO, the regression-line cross that triggers entries, and Sniper, the short-timeframe trade style that combines all four ideas into a single high-volatility shot defended by a tight risk box.

The 5 concept pages

  • Frog — An intraday trading system that uses descriptive statistics — specifically a volatility “Frog box” (30-day range stddev) — to separate signal from noise and find symbols poised to make larger-than-normal moves.
  • MMRB — The Minimum Manageable Risk Box (MMRB) is the smallest stop distance — about one-tenth of the 30-day average range — that you can defend without being shaken out by noise, so a -1R loss is a tiny fraction of your account.
  • PPEA — PPEA — Plan, Prepare, Execute, Assess — is Ken Long’s top-level four-phase process that governs every trading action, from system design to the next live trade.
  • RLCO — Regression Line Cross Over — the moment a fast regression line (RL10) crosses its slower partner (RL30) outside the Bollinger “river,” giving an early read on whether a trend will continue or reverse.
  • Sniper — Sniper is Ken Long’s name for short-timeframe, ruthlessly-managed intraday trades taken on a single high-volatility target using a small, well-defined setup (often an opening-range break) with a tight risk box.

Cross-links across the 5 pages:

  • ppea → frog: Once the four-phase loop is in your hands, the frog system shows what Prepare and Execute look like on a real intraday chart.
  • ppea → sniper: sniper trades are PPEA compressed into minutes — the Plan and Prepare are done before the bell so Execute can be ruthless.
  • frog → mmrb: The same 30-day range that defines the Frog box also sizes the mmrb — about one-tenth of that range is the smallest stop you can defend.
  • frog → rlco: Inside the Frog box, an rlco cross is the trigger that tells you the larger-than-normal move is starting.
  • rlco → frog: RLCO is most reliable when it fires on a symbol the frog system has already flagged as primed to move.
  • rlco → mmrb: Once an RLCO fires, the mmrb tells you exactly how far the stop sits so a wrong cross costs only -1R.
  • sniper → mmrb: Every Sniper trade is built around an mmrb stop — the tight risk box is what lets you take the shot at all.
  • sniper → rlco: Many Sniper entries use an rlco cross as the pull-the-trigger signal on the opening-range break.
  • mmrb → frog: The MMRB is derived from the same 30-day range stddev that defines the frog box — one statistic, two uses.
  • mmrb → sniper: The MMRB is what makes a sniper trade defensible — without a tight, noise-aware stop the shot is just a guess.
