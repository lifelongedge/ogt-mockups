You are not the same as everyone else who walks in.

PATH A

New to trading. New to Ken.

You're motivated and you're ready, but the trading world is still mostly new to you. SGC works for you, but pace matters.

Honest check: if you haven't taken the Foundations course yet, do that in parallel. SGC builds on it; it's not a substitute.
  1. Read Foundation Session 2 — the intellectual lineage so you know which thinkers Ken stands on
  2. Read Foundation Session 3 + PPEA wiki — the master scaffold
  3. Browse RLCO — watch any 2 video moments, don't try to absorb all 8
  4. Show up to your first session as a listener. Take notes. Don't ask questions yet.
  5. Write a one-paragraph "what I noticed" reflection after
⏱ ~3 hours over week 1 · expect this to compound for months
PATH B

Newer trader, but you know Ken's world.

Long-time Patreon listener / Foundations course alum. You know the vocabulary; you haven't put enough reps in to make it automatic.

  1. Skip the lineage. Go straight to PPEA + MMRB — the two that govern execution
  2. Show up to your first live session and ask a question — you've earned the privilege of asking; the cohort needs to hear from new voices
  3. Pick one technique to practice this week — RLCO OR Frog, not both
  4. Paper-trade or sized-down live, with the technique as the framing lens
⏱ ~2 hours over week 1 · biggest lift is showing up and being present

PATH C

Experienced trader. New to Ken's vocabulary.

You've put on real trades for real years. You're here because something in Ken's framing — process discipline, the RLCO lens, his weekend reports — caught your attention. You don't need the basics. You need the translation.

  1. Read the Library index — a 5-min vocabulary primer on RLCO, MMRB, PPEA, Frog, Sniper
  2. Skim RLCO + PPEA — these are the two that Ken treats as load-bearing; the rest sits on top
  3. Show up to your first live session with your hardest current unresolved question ready to ask
  4. After the session, identify one thing Ken does that contradicts your current system. That's where the value is — not where you already agree.
  5. Map Ken's vocabulary onto your existing playbook. Where do they overlap? Where do they fight?
⏱ ~90 min over week 1 · the live session does the heavy lifting
PATH D

Experienced trader. Long-time Owl Group community.

You've been around. You upgraded to SGC for the direct-coaching loop, not the content. You know the wiki concepts already; you've heard Ken explain RLCO a hundred times.

  1. Bring your single most-unresolved strategic question to the next live session — the one you've been chewing on for months
  2. Browse the Library for any concept where Ken's framing has shifted recently (the wiki shows you the most-recent video moments first)
  3. Use the live cohort as your sounding board — your experience is valuable to other students; the back-and-forth is the product
  4. Optional: volunteer to share a trade review at an upcoming session
⏱ ~30 min of prep · 100% leverage on live time

The four paths converge after a few weeks. The 2×2 is just for finding your starting point.

Common questions

New-student FAQ

How do I prepare for my first session?
Read Foundation Sessions 2 + 3 (above). That's it. Ken doesn't expect you to have read his book cover-to-cover. He DOES expect you to be familiar with PPEA so the live framing exercises make sense. Bring a notebook (paper or digital) — the cohort takes notes the old way.
What if I can't make a live session?
Every session is recorded and lives in the Patreon archive within 24 hours. The Library will surface the most-relevant clips automatically over time. Showing up live is preferable — that's where Q&A happens — but the asynchronous path works.
What if my path (above) feels wrong for me?
The 2×2 is a starting point, not a label. If you sit between two quadrants, pick the more cautious of the two — over-preparing is cheap, under-preparing wastes live-session time. After 2-3 weeks the paths converge anyway; you'll find your own pace.
Where do I ask questions between sessions?
Patreon comments + the cohort chat (link in your welcome email). Save your highest-value question for the live session — that's where Ken's attention compounds. Quick logistical questions belong in chat.
How long until I "get it"?
Ken's honest answer: most students need 2-3 years of consistent engagement before the frameworks become automatic. SGC is built for the long climb. If you're looking for a quick edge, this is the wrong room.
What does the Library cover vs. just watching all of Patreon?
The Patreon archive is 300+ unindexed videos. The Library is the same content, but organized by concept — Ken's clearest explanation of MMRB across all 300 videos lives on one page, in order, with timestamps. You'll still watch raw sessions for the live cohort feel; the Library is for "when did Ken explain X?" — that's the gold the archive alone can't deliver.
