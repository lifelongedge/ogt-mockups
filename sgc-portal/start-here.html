Your first week

Five steps. Roughly 3 hours total. Do them in order.

Don't try to absorb everything at once. The point of the first week is to land in the cohort with enough foundation that Ken's first live session lands with full context.

  1. Pick a frame: read Foundation Session 215 min

    The "Top books, thinkers, practitioners who influenced Ken's work" map. You don't need to read all those books — you need the map. It tells you what kind of traders Ken stands on the shoulders of and what intellectual lineage you're entering.

    Open Foundation Sessions (coming · for now, ask your cohort coach for the PDF)

  2. Learn the master framework: read Foundation Session 3 + the PPEA wiki page25 min

    Plan · Prepare · Execute · Assess. Every other technique Ken teaches sits on this scaffold. If you skip PPEA, every later session feels like loose tactics. With PPEA, everything fits.

    Read PPEA wiki →

  3. Add the lens: read the RLCO concept page30 min

    The Regression Line Cross Over is Ken's primary chart-reading lens. Once you see it, you can't unsee it. The wiki page has 8 video moments where Ken teaches it from different angles — watch any 2-3.

    Read RLCO wiki →

  4. Show up to your first live session60-90 min

    Live participation is the whole point of SGC. Even if you only listen, hearing Ken work through a current trade with your cohort is worth 10x reading. Don't wait until you "feel ready" — you won't.

    See the schedule ↓ · Get the Zoom link

  5. Pick one technique and practice it for the rest of the week30-45 min

    Don't try to use everything at once. Pick MMRB (sizing) OR Frog (signal vs noise) OR Sniper (execution) — one concept. Use it to frame every trade idea you look at, even hypothetical ones. The point is repetition, not P&L.

    MMRB · Frog · Sniper

Common questions

New-student FAQ

How do I prepare for my first session?
Read Foundation Sessions 2 + 3 (above). That's it. Ken doesn't expect you to have read his book cover-to-cover. He DOES expect you to be familiar with PPEA so the live framing exercises make sense. Bring a notebook (paper or digital) — the cohort takes notes the old way.
What if I can't make a live session?
Every session is recorded and lives in the Patreon archive within 24 hours. The Library will surface the most-relevant clips automatically over time. Showing up live is preferable — that's where Q&A happens — but the asynchronous path works.
I'm completely new to trading. Am I in the right place?
SGC assumes you've placed at least a handful of trades and have a brokerage account. If you haven't, start with the foundational Foundations course first — that's the on-ramp. SGC is the live-coaching layer on top of that foundation.
Where do I ask questions between sessions?
Patreon comments + the cohort chat (link in your welcome email). Save your highest-value question for the live session — that's where Ken's attention compounds. Quick logistical questions belong in chat.
How long until I "get it"?
Ken's honest answer: most students need 2-3 years of consistent engagement before the frameworks become automatic. SGC is built for the long climb. If you're looking for a quick edge, this is the wrong room.
What does the Library cover vs. just watching all of Patreon?
The Patreon archive is 300+ unindexed videos. The Library is the same content, but organized by concept — Ken's clearest explanation of MMRB across all 300 videos lives on one page, in order, with timestamps. You'll still watch raw sessions for the live cohort feel; the Library is for "when did Ken explain X?" — that's the gold the archive alone can't deliver.
