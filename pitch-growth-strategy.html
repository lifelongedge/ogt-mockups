The knowledge base is extraordinary. Root Causes

Three structural problems, not three marketing problems

1. No Daily Operator

Dr. Long creates. Sundeep and Mark build infrastructure. None of us have capacity to operate a daily content-to-revenue pipeline. The business needs a third role: someone (or something) that wakes up every morning, processes the latest report, builds a briefing, writes an email, and sends it — every single day, without being asked.

2. Content-Product Gap

The bookstore has 36 products spanning $4.97 to $197. The email sequences teach concepts. But nothing connects them. A student reading about MACD Seasons never sees a link to the Glossary. A subscriber learning about position sizing never encounters the Fieldguide. The content and the commerce exist in separate worlds.

3. Interpretation Gap

Dr. Long's daily reports are auto-generated — 30+ pages of MACD seasons, regression lines, and system signals across 300+ symbols. This is the raw data that powers his trading decisions. But for email distribution and new subscriber acquisition, the reports need a lighter-weight summary layer: what changed today, which concepts are in play, and where to go deeper.

The Approach

An AI system that operates the content-to-revenue pipeline daily, without human intervention

A complete operational layer that performs the daily work of a growth team:

  • Ingest — Parse Dr. Long's daily/weekend reports automatically when they arrive
  • Translate — Compute day-over-day diffs and generate plain-English "What Changed" summaries
  • Publish — Build a daily briefing (the free product that demonstrates value)
  • Email — Send a concept spotlight with today's data and a contextual product CTA to 620 subscribers
  • Measure — Track opens, clicks, purchases. Adjust concept rotation based on engagement data.

This will run on Cloud Run. One codebase. One deploy. The engine prototype exists and generates briefings from ingested reports. What remains is automating the daily pipeline end-to-end: auto-ingest, email send, and commerce links.

Email Strategy

30 concept emails, 3 rounds, contextual CTAs

We pre-build 30 emails covering 10 core concepts. Each email has three components:

Concept Spotlight

~200 words teaching one idea from Dr. Long's methodology — in his voice, using his analogies. Each one delivers standalone value.

"What Changed" Section

Dynamically injected from the day's diff data. MACD season transitions, setup alerts, volatility signals. This is what makes each email timely — the concept is evergreen, the data is today's.

Product CTA

One relevant product linked at the bottom — a natural extension of the concept. "If this resonated, the Glossary defines every term I just used." Price ranges from $4.97 to $97.95.

The 30 emails rotate in 3 rounds of 10:

  • Round 1 (Days 1-10): Core concept introductions — teach the vocabulary
  • Round 2 (Days 11-20): New angle — Collapsing Dragon deep dive, real trade walkthroughs, the morning script
  • Round 3 (Days 21-30): Student stories — "Tom was trading against the season," "Eric's position sizing crisis"

Hypotheses

Four claims we're making — and how we'll know if we're wrong

H1: The delta briefing is a viable lead magnet

People who see a delta briefing — an abridged "What Changed" summary drawn from Dr. Long's full report will find it valuable enough to subscribe for daily email delivery.
Test: Share the briefing link with existing 620 subscribers and track email signup rate from the delta briefing page.
Success: >5% of briefing visitors sign up for email. >50 new subscribers in first 30 days.
Failure signal: <2% signup rate after 30 days = the delta briefing isn't compelling enough on its own. Revisit format.
Testable by: Day 30

H2: Concept emails drive product discovery

Teaching a concept and then linking to a relevant product creates meaningful click-through — not because the email is a sales pitch, but because the concept creates genuine curiosity about the source material.
Test: Track CTA click rate across 30 concept emails. Compare to industry baseline (2-5% for educational email).
Success: >3% CTA click rate. At least 1 in 20 clicks converts to a purchase.
Failure signal: <1% click rate after 2 full rotations (60 days) = CTAs aren't relevant or products aren't priced right.
Testable by: Day 60

H3: AI-generated content passes the Dr. Long test

Emails written from the 2M-word knowledge base, using Dr. Long's analogies and voice patterns, will be indistinguishable from content he would write himself — or at minimum, approved with minor edits.
Test: Dr. Long reviews first 10 emails and scores each: Approved / Minor edits / Major rewrite / Reject.
Success: 8/10 approved or minor edits. Zero rejects.
Failure signal: >3 major rewrites = voice model needs recalibration from more source material.
Testable by: Day 7

H4: Daily consistency compounds into growth

Sending a valuable email every single day — not weekly, not "when we have something" — builds a habit loop that converts casual subscribers into paying members. The daily cadence is the product.
Test: Compare 30-day retention and open rates of daily sends vs. the current sporadic pattern (12 sends in 10 months).
Success: Open rate stabilizes at >35% after 30 days. Unsubscribe rate <0.5% per send.
Failure signal: Open rate declines below 20% after 14 days = daily is too frequent. Test 3x/week.
Testable by: Day 30

Risks & Mitigations

What could go wrong

Risk

  • Voice mismatch — Emails don't sound like Dr. Long. Students notice. Trust erodes.
  • Factual errors — AI gets a trading concept wrong. A student acts on bad information.
  • Email fatigue — Daily sends annoy subscribers. Open rates collapse. List shrinks.
  • Gumroad friction — Current bookstore is on Gumroad. Adding Stripe creates two checkout experiences.
  • Content runs dry — 30 emails covers 30 days. What happens on day 31?

Mitigation

  • Approval gate — Dr. Long reviews first 10. All emails cite his knowledge base verbatim. No fabricated claims.
  • Facts-only policy — Emails teach concepts, never give trade recommendations. Every claim traces to source material.
  • Fallback cadence — If open rate drops below 20% after 14 days, switch to 3x/week. Kit already has active/digest segmentation.
  • Phase Stripe in — Week 7-8. Keep Gumroad links until Stripe is tested. No disruption to existing buyers.
  • Rotation + expansion — After 30, build 30 more from Foundations (163 lessons) and SGC recordings (180+ sessions). Source material is effectively infinite.
Competitive Position

Why this particular approach, for this particular business

Three structural advantages that make OGT different from every other trading education business trying AI:

  • The content already exists. The AI operates the distribution pipeline — it doesn't invent the content. Dr. Long's 40-year methodology is the source material. We're building the channel that gets it to the right people at the right time.
  • The data is proprietary and daily. Dr. Long's reports are auto-generated from his own systems — MACD seasons, regression lines, and signals across 300+ symbols. No competitor has this data. The daily briefing can't be replicated by someone with a ChatGPT subscription because the underlying data feed doesn't exist anywhere else.
  • The cost structure inverts. Traditional trading educators hire content teams, video editors, email marketers, and community managers. Each additional student increases operational load. This system has near-zero marginal cost. The 1,000th subscriber costs the same as the 1st.

We're not competing on AI sophistication. We're competing on the depth and authenticity of Dr. Long's methodology, delivered at a frequency and consistency that wasn't possible before.
