The Collapsing Dragon Pathway

MACD Seasons Collapsing Dragon SSC SGC Session — May 2024 · 2:00

Dr. Long explains the Harsh Winter → SSC → Collapsing Dragon pathway — how a large directional move puts a symbol "in play" and what triggers each pattern in sequence.

"...with the harsh winter as the filter, which says I'm ready to get in. So that's step one on that pathway. I need harsh winter first, then SSC, CODA2, and Collapsing Dragon. The second pathway says instead of waiting to see a harsh winter, what I'm gonna do is I'm gonna see the market closed here on it yesterday, and then there was a big gap in either direction. And the fact that there was a large gap substitutes for the requirement of seeing a harsh winter..."

Position Sizing Psychology

Position Sizing Risk Psychology SGC Session — Hougaard Press · 2:00

Dr. Long coaches a student through the psychology of scaling up position size — the tension between the gut and the math, and how to calibrate risk tolerance incrementally.

"...if I'm at 140 and I think I should be at 1,000, maybe I can go to 150, and then get some evidence, and then 175, and then 200, and then some logical progression. And keep checking in with your gut to saying, how does it feel now to be trading at that level of risk? At the same time, we also have to make sure that the gut understands that opportunity cost is there, too..."

Tracking Trades Independently

R-Multiples Expectancy SGC Session — Calculating R · 2:00

Clear, authoritative explanation of why each entry must be tracked as a separate trade — hypothesis testing, positive expectancy, and the discipline of measuring your edge.

"...the quality of the setup says I buy at this level according to a rule and I get out at that level according to a rule. So the rule doesn't care how much money you have on it or how many shares. It's simply measuring the result of that hypothesis. Your hypothesis is this thing is a good trade, good enough to trade, favorable reward to risk ratio, positive expectancy..."

Volatility Regimes

Volatility Market Classification SGC Session — Cohort 3, Jan 2025 · 2:00

The internet bubble analogy — how volatility regimes shift and what was once "holy cow" becomes "new normal." Three regimes of quiet, normal, and volatile defined over 180 periods.

"...daily frog box was 0.75 and then after the internet explosion, it settled down to a new normal and the new daily ATR percentage was 1.5% and it felt absolutely normal. But if I'd gone back pre internet explosion and collapse, when it was at 0.75%, if we'd had a day that was 1.5%, we just said, holy cow, something huge..."
